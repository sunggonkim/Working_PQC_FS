\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Interpretation of the placement result}
\label{sec:disc_tradeoff}

The useful result is asymmetry, not aggregate ``GPU speedup.''  CPU/OpenSSL fits AES-GCM data writes, while GPU/cuPQC helps slack-tolerant ML-KEM maintenance.  Under UMA contention, secure file encryption needs admission and fallback rules that see both cryptographic role and foreground storage slack; AEGIS-Q trades peak background throughput for explicit publication, placement, QoS, and recovery boundaries.

\subsection{Security limits}
\label{sec:disc_energy}

The per-file envelope is authenticated and block records are AEAD-protected, but the mount password remains the root credential.  There is no hardware-backed credential release, audited rotation protocol, privileged-attacker defense, or side-channel evaluation.  TPM probes cover fail-closed replay-after-advance, not persistent PCR-bound key release.

\subsection{Evaluation limits and roadmap}
\label{sec:disc_roadmap}

The primitive measurements cannot establish steady-state throughput, energy efficiency, or portability.  The frozen matrix covers plaintext, gocryptfs, dm-crypt, and AEGIS-Q; fscrypt is environment-blocked by kernel/filesystem proof and is not reported as a throughput row.  The matrix exposes authenticated-publication cost; it is not a full fscrypt, fs-verity, dm-integrity, OP-TEE, SPDK, or GPUDirect Storage comparison or free storage path.

AEGIS-Q is not a general POSIX filesystem: read/write/\texttt{fsync}, scoped rename, directory \texttt{fsync}, no-open hard links, relative symlinks, and bounded writer races are evaluated; shared \texttt{mmap}, full link lifecycle, open-subtree retargeting, arbitrary byte-range transactions, and application-level concurrency are not deployment claims.

The crash story retains daemon crashes, SQLite/dbm recovery, append-log/cache-manifest remount, syscall-exact app-crash, and loopback ext4/device-mapper accepted-state recovery.  It still lacks physical power-loss, kernel-crash, drive-cache, broad workload-suite, non-NVIDIA UMA, and side-channel evidence.

Kernel assistance is future work: D/J/C barrier issuance could move to a helper, but only if a same-format kernel path preserves the recovery oracle; Merkle/integrity jobs must stay independent, batched, slack-tolerant, and format-compatible.

\subsection{Deployment takeaway}
The evaluated envelope is local: SQLite, elastic background writes, append-log/cache-manifest remounts, authenticated FUSE, kernel/FUSE-daemon trust, and Jetson/CUDA/TPM dependencies.  The SQLite point is the same storage-pressure case summarized by Figure~\ref{fig:first_page_qos} and Table~\ref{tab:qos_sqlite_recovery}, not a separate deployment anecdote.  The portable lesson is the policy split: CPU AES-GCM publication, slack-gated PQC maintenance, and executor-local managed memory.
