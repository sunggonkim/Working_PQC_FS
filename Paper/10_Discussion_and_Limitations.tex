\section{Discussion and Limitations}
\label{sec:discussion}

The useful result is asymmetry, not aggregate ``GPU speedup.''  CPU/OpenSSL fits AES-GCM data writes, while GPU/cuPQC helps slack-tolerant ML-KEM maintenance.  AEGIS-Q trades peak background throughput for explicit publication, placement, QoS, and recovery boundaries; stale telemetry costs elastic progress, not the CPU/D/J/C foreground path.

The trust model is kernel/FUSE-daemon trust plus backing-format checks.  The mount password remains the root credential; there is no hardware-backed credential release, audited rotation, privileged-attacker defense, or side-channel evaluation.  TPM probes cover fail-closed replay-after-advance only, not persistent PCR-bound key release.  The evaluated envelope is local: SQLite, elastic writes, append-log/cache-manifest remounts, authenticated FUSE, and Jetson/CUDA/TPM dependencies.

The primitive measurements cannot establish steady-state throughput, energy efficiency, or portability beyond one Jetson-class accelerated UMA stack.  The frozen matrix covers plaintext, gocryptfs, dm-crypt, and AEGIS-Q; fscrypt is environment-blocked by proof and not reported as throughput.  The matrix exposes authenticated-publication cost, not a full fscrypt, fs-verity, dm-integrity, OP-TEE, SPDK, or GPUDirect Storage comparison.

AEGIS-Q is not a general POSIX filesystem: read/write/\texttt{fsync}, scoped rename, directory \texttt{fsync}, no-open hard links, symlinks, and bounded writer races are evaluated; shared \texttt{mmap}, full link lifecycle, open-subtree retargeting, arbitrary byte-range transactions, and application-level concurrency are not deployment claims.

The crash story retains daemon crashes, SQLite/dbm recovery, append-log/cache-manifest remounts, syscall-exact app-crash, and loopback ext4/device-mapper recovery.  It assumes lower-filesystem \texttt{fdatasync} ordering for \texttt{.pqcdata} before \texttt{.pqcmeta} plus checkpoint xattr visibility; reordering after acknowledged sync or lost xattrs are outside the selected crash model.  It still lacks physical power-loss, kernel-crash, drive-cache, broad workload-suite, cross-SoC, and side-channel evidence.

AEGIS-Q fixes 4~KiB authenticated records and coalesces larger writes into bounded 512~KiB batches; 16/64/128~KiB chunks change amortization, not nonce/AAD format.  The remaining strict cost is barrier publication.

Kernel assistance is future work: D/J/C barrier issuance, \texttt{io\_uring} submission, or fscrypt-adjacent hooks could reduce per-sync overhead only if a same-format kernel path preserves the recovery oracle.
